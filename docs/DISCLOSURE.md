# Responsibility, disclosure & conflict of interest

## What CiteVahti is

CiteVahti is a **local-first citation-support audit tool for manuscript claims**. It
helps authors test whether the cited evidence supports each claim before submission.

## What it does — and does not — certify

Use of CiteVahti **does not certify** scientific truth, manuscript quality, publication
suitability, or the absence of citation problems. CiteVahti provides **structured
decision support and an audit trail** — it records *your* judgment, with provenance.

**Final responsibility remains with the human author, reviewer, editor, or
institution.** CiteVahti is an aid, not an authority.

## Conflict of interest

CiteVahti is developed by the author. Any publication, review, teaching, or evaluation
that uses CiteVahti **should disclose the tool use and the developer relationship where
relevant.**

## Suggested disclosure language

For a methods section, acknowledgements, or a reviewer note, you can adapt:

> *"Pre-submission citation support was performed with CiteVahti, an open-source,
> local-first citation-support audit tool. CiteVahti was used for structured
> decision support and audit logging only; it does not certify the validity of any
> claim, and final responsibility for all citations rests with the authors."*

If you have a relationship with the tool's developer, add it — for example:

> *"One of the authors is the developer of CiteVahti."*
